# Bibliography and modeling

Dynamical modeling, network dynamics or agentic theory used for a the study of corporation's evolution and behavior is something rarely , if not at all , studied academically. This is due to many reasons. The most obvious reason is the number of different parameters and relations between different objects. Studying a corporation on employee level creates a number of personalised and unique parameters. All those different values are not always static, meaning that the system is also non-autonomous. Then we have the random events that complicate the processess and can lead to periods of flourishing and periods of shrinkage. The first model was based around the basic theory of creating and running a tech company. Although it is something a person of above average intellect and adequate education can surmise, let's be strictly academic and provide references. The basis of the model's structure is the AGILE's approach to interpersonal cooperation for the advancement of production scrums/sprints (https://gist.github.com/cboone/04511733a0985a99b80069c73b6557a0 - quite an extensive and educational collection). This means that agents become more productive (and more profitable) the more they interact. This also one of the issues we are studying a little more in depth. Based on journalism on the issue of "quiet quitting", "job hoping" and "quiet firing" we have designed metrics that monitor the fatigue and employee willingness to leave the company. The details of the parameters and setting the simulation up are inv]cluded in the guidebook for each different model version.
